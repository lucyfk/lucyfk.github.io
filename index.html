Journal Article · Physiological Measurement · 2021
Video-Based Non-Contact Monitoring of Respiratory Rate and Chest Indrawing in Children with Pneumonia
Ferdous Karim Lucy, Khadiza Tun Suha, Sumaiya Tabassum Dipty, Md Sharjis Ibne Wadud, Muhammad Abdul Kadir  ·  Physiological Measurement, Vol. 42, IOP Publishing, 2021
Overview
Pneumonia is the leading infectious cause of death in children under five worldwide. WHO identifies fast breathing and chest indrawing as the key clinical indicators requiring antibiotic treatment. No objective, non-contact method for detecting chest indrawing existed prior to this work. This study developed a novel smartphone-based system for simultaneous, non-intrusive monitoring of both indicators — without disturbing the child.
Methodology
Respiratory signals were extracted by analysing mean RGB colour components in video frames captured over the chest-abdominal region. A Butterworth bandpass filter removed noise, and Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) identified the dominant breathing frequency to calculate respiratory rate. Chest indrawing was detected by comparing the phase difference between respiratory signals from two anatomically defined Regions of Interest — the lower chest wall (ROI-1) and the central abdomen (ROI-2). The algorithm was implemented in MATLAB and evaluated across 50 trials on 5 healthy and 5 pneumonia children.

Significance
The method reliably distinguished chest indrawing from normal breathing with high statistical significance (Mann-Whitney U test, p < 0.001). Being non-intrusive and fully objective, it holds strong potential for pneumonia diagnosis in low-resource settings. The methodology may further extend to sleep apnoea, breathing pattern disorders, and respiratory-gated radiotherapy.
